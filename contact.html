Where This Story Actually Starts

I'm a leadership practitioner with 30 years of experience — but that's not really where this story starts.

It starts with a gay boy growing up Baptist in Texas, learning early that parts of him didn't fit the world he was handed. It starts with running through the dark with his mother. With watching the people he loved struggle. With building a version of himself that was acceptable to everyone around him — and calling that survival.

What I didn't understand then is what I understand now: I wasn't broken. I was adapting. And that adaptation shaped everything about how I lead, how I work, and how I show up in the world.

"For decades, many of us have been handed blueprints for what success looks like, what leadership looks like, what a good life means — built by systems that weren't designed with all of us in mind."

— Jessie Heath

The Career Behind the Curtain

I was the first in my immediate family to graduate college, the first to reach a level of stability I once couldn't have imagined. I've built a career, a family — a husband, two kids, a dog, a cat, and the full beautiful chaos that comes with all of it.

And I've done much of it carrying things I kept off the shelf: PTSD, chronic depression, social anxiety, imposter syndrome. I've managed them, grown through them, and they've made me who I am.

Human Work is where I stop keeping those things on the shelf. It's where I connect the life to the work — because no matter how hard we try to separate them, they were never separate to begin with.

About the Human Work Project

Human Work is a blog, a training series, a speaking platform, and a community built around one core idea: the life you've lived and the leader you are aren't two different things.

Most of us found ways to fit ourselves into blueprints rather than question them. Human Work is here to examine those blueprints — and help leaders build something better in their place.

The neuroscience backs this up. Our brains are wired for the patterns we've practiced, the environments we were raised in, and the identities we've constructed to survive. Changing leadership behavior isn't a training problem. It's a human problem. And it requires human solutions.

How I Work

A few honest notes: I'm a talker more than a writer, so I use dictation and AI tools to help me put words on the page without losing the voice behind them. I'm also a busy human — two kids, a working household, a full career — so this grows as life allows.

There's also a book, keynote work, and development offerings in progress, because my brain doesn't really have an off switch. What I do know is this: I spent a long time waiting until things were perfect before sharing them. I've decided that's over.

This is the work. Welcome to it.
